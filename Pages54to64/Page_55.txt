STEP 1: HAZARD IDENTIFICATION

Lexical Hazards:

* Menighed / Menigheden: must be rendered consistently as “the Church” (not merely “congregation”), with ecclesiological weight.
* Staber: archaic metaphor (staff/support/pillar); must retain pastoral-biblical resonance.
* Forløser, Brudgom, Pant, Barneret, Barneliv: doctrinally loaded terms requiring stable theological English.
* Aand / Helligaand: must consistently distinguish Spirit/Holy Spirit with Trinitarian clarity.
* Beskuelse: not mere “seeing,” but contemplative sight as opposed to faith.
* Ringhed, Fornedrelse, Skrøbelighed: must preserve biblical humility language.

Syntactic Hazards:

* Long periodic sentences with semicolons and accumulative predicates.
* Triadic Trinitarian parallelisms.
* Logical pivots introduced by “Men” that must not be weakened.
* Rhetorical repetition of “synlig/usynlig.”

Theological Hazards:

* Justification by faith alone.
* Regeneration and adoption.
* Means of grace (Word and Sacraments).
* Visible/invisible Church distinction without dividing the Church.
* Revelation as prerequisite for faith.

Voice Hazards:

* Sermonic cadence and confessional clarity.
* Exhortational tone addressed to conscience.
* Biblical density that must not be flattened.

STEP 2: IDIOM ANALYSIS

* “Samfundet i den Helligaand, de helliges Samfund”: Creedal language (Communio Sanctorum).
* “Liv er skjult med Kristus i Gud”: Colossians 3:3 echo.
* “Stad paa Bjerget”: Matthew 5:14.
* “Den enbaarne Søn, som er i Faderens Skjød”: John 1:18.
* “Gives ved synlige Midler”: Lutheran means-of-grace idiom.
* “Pant”: earnest/pledge (Ephesians 1:14).

Literal rendering must be guided by KJV diction to preserve recognizability.

STEP 3: ARGUMENT STRUCTURE IDENTIFICATION

* Trinitarian foundation of the Church.
* Definition of the Church as justified, regenerated, adopted people.
* Clarification: the Church’s glory is an object of faith, not sight.
* Principle: faith requires revelation.
* Application to God’s self-revelation.
* Application to the Church as both invisible and visible.
* Analogy between God’s revelation in Christ and the Church’s manifestation.
* Concluding assertion grounded in Scripture.

STEP 4: BIBLICAL ECHO IDENTIFICATION

* John 1:18 – revelation of the Father.
* Romans 1:20 – God’s invisible attributes perceived.
* Colossians 3:3 – hidden life in God.
* Matthew 5:14 – city on a hill.
* John 14:9 – seeing the Son, seeing the Father.
* Apostles’ Creed – Communion of Saints.

STEP 5: VOICE INTENSITY PRESERVATION

* No softening of doctrinal claims.
* Preserve declarative force and confidence.
* Maintain solemn, confessional seriousness.

STEP 6: MODAL FORCE PRESERVATION

* “kan intet,” “ingen kan,” “maa fastholde”: must retain necessity and impossibility.

STEP 7: CONSISTENT THEOLOGICAL TERM RENDERING

* Menighed → Church
* Helligaand → Holy Spirit
* Ord og Sacramenter → Word and Sacraments
* Aabenbaret → revealed
* Retfærdiggjort → justified
* Gjenfødt → born again / regenerated

STEP 8: METAPHOR INTEGRITY PRESERVATION

* Staff/support, light, city on a hill, hidden life.
* Do not explain away imagery.

STEP 9: TONE CONTRAST PRESERVATION

* Distinction between glory hidden and manifestation in weakness.
* Contrast between invisibility and visibility.

STEP 10: ORAL CADENCE AWARENESS

* Retain long sentences with controlled rhythm.
* Preserve parallelism and repetition.

God Himself is the Founder of the Church; He is her Father, He is her Staff and Support; the Son is her Redeemer, her King, her Head, her Bridegroom; the Spirit is her Life, her Freedom, her Advocate, her Pledge. The Church herself is a gathering of human beings whom God has justified through faith in His Son, born again unto the life of the Spirit and granted the right of sonship and the life of children, an inheritance in heaven with the Son and the eternal life of love in the Spirit.

But when we thus speak of the Church as the fellowship in the Holy Spirit, the communion of saints, and of the glory of this fellowship, then we are speaking of invisible and eternal things, which are the object of faith, not of sight. Yet nothing can be the object of faith which has not been revealed. No one can believe in God if God were only invisible. But because God has been revealed in the flesh, because the only-begotten Son, who is in the bosom of the Father, has made Him known, therefore faith is possible. The invisible, upon which faith relies, is also visible through its revelation. The Apostle Paul even says expressly that God’s invisible being, His eternal power and Godhead, are perceived from the creation of the world. So it is also with the communion of saints.

The Church: she is invisible, because her life is hidden with Christ in God; she is visible, because her life, God’s own Holy Spirit, is given to her through visible means, the Word and the Sacraments; visible, because she consists of truly visible human beings; visible, because she possesses recognizable marks and indeed is a shining light in the darkness of the world, a city set upon a hill that cannot be hidden.

The Church is therefore both invisible and visible, just as God Himself is invisible and visible (see John 14:9: He that hath seen Me hath seen the Father). Just as no one could believe in God if He were not revealed, so no one can believe in the communion of saints if it is not revealed. God has revealed Himself in His Son, who Himself is the Word of God; the Church reveals herself in believing human beings, who live by the Word and the Sacraments a spiritual life, rich in the fruits of mercy. And just as God’s revelation in the Son is in humiliation, in lowliness, in poverty, within the bounds of time and space, so also the Church’s revelation is in frailty, in inconspicuousness, in contempt, bound to definite times and definite places. Yet we must hold fast that just as it is the one and the same true God who is invisible and revealed, so it is also the one and the same Church of God that is invisible and visible; for thus says the Word of God: Ye are the light of the world; a city that is set on a hill cannot be hidden.
